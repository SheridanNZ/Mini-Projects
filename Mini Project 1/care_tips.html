Why Adopt?

So you've made the wonderful decision to give a shelter cat a forever home – that's amazing! In New Zealand, countless pets have been abandoned, mistreated, or left without owners who can care for them. These animals are all hoping for a second chance in a loving home. Whether you're adopting a playful kitten or an adult cat with a well-established personality, there are several important factors to consider before welcoming your new companion. This guide will help you understand what you'll need to prepare for your new furry family member.

Adopting a cat is one of the most rewarding experiences. Here are some reasons why you should consider adopting:

  • Save a Life: You’re giving a cat a second chance at happiness.
  • Support a Local Shelter: Your adoption fee helps support our ongoing efforts to rescue and care for animals.
  • Find the Perfect Match: Our adoption team works with you to ensure that you’re matched with a cat that fits your lifestyle.
  • Healthy Cats: All our cats are vaccinated, spayed/neutered, and microchipped before adoption.

Finding the Purrr-fect Cat for You

One of the most important aspects of adopting a cat is ensuring that their personality is a good fit for your family. At Meow & Furever, we provide detailed backgrounds on our cats, along with guidance on which ones might best suit your household. Finding the right match is key—not only for your family's dynamic but also for the cat's well-being. It's essential that the cat’s temperament aligns with your home environment. For example, if a cat isn’t fond of dogs, it wouldn’t be a good fit for a household with a dog. We’re here to help ensure that the cat you adopt will thrive in their new home and be the perfect companion for you and your family.

Frequently Asked Questions

We’ve compiled a list of common questions to help you with the adoption process:

Q: How do I know which cat is the right fit for me?
A: Browse our cat profiles and read about their personalities. If you're unsure, our team can help guide you based on your preferences and lifestyle.
Q: What happens after I submit my adoption application?
A: We will review your application, and if everything looks good, we'll contact you for an interview and to arrange a 'meet and greet' session at our facility.
Q: Do I need to pay an adoption fee?
A: Yes, we charge an adoption fee to cover the costs of veterinary care, vaccinations, spaying/neutering, and other expenses. This fee varies depending on the cat's age and other factors, but it's an essential part of our efforts to care for and rehome animals.
Q: Can I adopt a cat if I live in an apartment or have other pets?
A: Absolutely! We believe that every home can be suitable for a cat. However, we do ask that you provide us with information about your living situation (such if renting approval from Landlord to have pets, or proof of ownership if not renting) and any other pets you may have so we can help find the best match for your home.
Q: Can I meet the cat before I apply for adoption?
A: Yes, we encourage prospective adopters to meet the cat in person. If you're interested in meeting a specific cat, please contact us to arrange a visit and ensure it's available for viewing.
Q: Do you provide any post-adoption support?
A: Yes, we offer post-adoption support to help with any transition issues that may arise. If you have any concerns about your cat's behavior, health, or settling into your home, don't hesitate to reach out to us.
